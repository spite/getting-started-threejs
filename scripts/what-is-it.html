A bit of history: - Coding and 3D graphics - GPU - OpenGL, OpenGL ES, WebGL - Web as a platform: development, deployment and extension Enter three.js - lightweight cross-browser JavaScript library - renderer agnostic, abstraction and encapsulation - github.com (april 2010) - mrdoob (Ricardo Cabello), supereggbert (Paul Runt) - alteredqualia (Branislav Ulicny), zz85 (Joshua Koo) - stackoverflow.com - examples ! Features - modular renderers - canvas 2D, canvas 3D (WebGL), SVG, CSS3D - scenes - fog - cameras - ortho and perspective, controllers - objects and LOD, loaders - meshes, particles, sprites, lines, ribbons, bones - binary, image, JSON and scene - lights and shadows - ambient, directional, point and spot - percentage closer filtering shadow mapping - geometry primitives and modifiers - plane, cube, sphere, torus, 3D text - lathe, extrude, tube - materials - Lambert, Phong, smooth shading, textured - animation - armatures, forward and inverse kinematics, morph and keyframe - shaders - GLSL vertex and fragment shaders - effects and postprocessing - lens flare, depth pass, blur, interlacing - anaglyph, cross-eyed and parallax barrier - tools, exporters/importers - Stats.js, WebGL Inspector, Three.js Inspector - Blender, openCTM, FBX, Max, and OBJ - utilities and helpers - time and 3D math functions - light, camera helpers
